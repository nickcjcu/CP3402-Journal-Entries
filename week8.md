# Week 8

## Learning Activities & Resources
This week, I focused on testing a learning method to see if it would improve my understanding compared to previous weeks.

**Hypothesis:**  
Reviewing documentation first, then applying it through coding, and finally reflecting on it will lead to better understanding than just learning while doing.

**Experiment:**  
I tested this by changing how I approached learning PHP concepts and general project work this week.

- Step One - I first read documentation and watched a short tutorial on the topic before doing any coding.  
- Step Two - I then applied what I learned by writing code and testing it myself.  
- Step Three - After completing the task, I reviewed the same resources again to reinforce my understanding and identify anything I missed.

To compare, I reflected on my previous approach in earlier weeks, where I mostly learned by jumping straight into the task and figuring things out as I went.

**Result:**  
The new method made it easier to understand concepts more clearly before starting. When coding, I made fewer mistakes and spent less time debugging basic issues. The final review step also helped reinforce what I learned and made it easier to explain concepts in my journal.

This suggests that the hypothesis is mostly true, as the structured approach improved both understanding and efficiency.

In addition to the experiment, I also continued preparing for the project presentation by reviewing what we have completed so far and thinking about how to clearly explain our work.

## Content Insights
This experiment showed that the order of learning activities has a direct impact on understanding. Starting with documentation provides a clear overview of concepts, which makes it easier to apply them during coding.

Applying knowledge immediately after learning helps reinforce concepts, while reviewing afterwards helps identify gaps in understanding. This creates a more complete learning cycle compared to just learning while doing.

I also noticed that this approach reduced confusion when working on tasks. Instead of guessing how something works, I had a clearer idea before starting, which improved efficiency.

## Career/Employability/Learning Insights
This week highlighted the importance of structured learning and continuous improvement. Being able to test and refine how I learn is a valuable skill, especially in a field like web development where new technologies are constantly introduced.

The experiment also showed that reflection is an important part of learning. Taking time to review and evaluate what worked well helped me improve my approach.

This is relevant to employability, as developers are expected to learn independently and adapt quickly. Having an effective learning strategy makes it easier to pick up new tools and technologies in the future.

Overall, this week helped me better understand how I learn and how to improve my efficiency when working on technical tasks.

# Week 3

## Learning Activities & Resources
This week, I focused on learning how to use WordPress by creating a simple version of my ActionBoost website. I used CloudAccess to set up a hosted WordPress site and worked directly on the live environment. Instead of building new content, I reused the structure and ideas from my Joomla site, which allowed me to focus more on understanding how WordPress works.

I selected the Twenty Twenty-One theme after researching different default themes. I initially considered using the newer Twenty Twenty-Five theme, but found that it uses a block-based editing system which made navigation editing more complicated. I preferred the older Twenty Twenty-One theme because it allowed me to manage menus and navigation through a more straightforward interface in the dashboard.

I created core pages such as Home, About Us, and Features, and practised linking them through the navigation menu. I explored how WordPress handles pages, menus, and themes, and compared this with how Joomla uses articles and menus. I also experimented with basic theme settings, layout adjustments, and content formatting.

To support my learning, I watched tutorials on WordPress basics, read documentation on themes and menus, and explored forums discussing differences between block themes and classic themes.

As part of this week, I also completed the group formation activity. I participated in short interview-style discussions with classmates, where we asked each other questions about working style, availability, communication, and expectations for the group project. I took notes during these discussions to reflect on what makes an effective team.

## Content Insights
WordPress provides a simpler and more beginner-friendly way to create websites compared to Joomla, especially when working with pages and themes. Creating pages such as Home, About Us, and Features was straightforward, and linking them to the navigation menu was easier than in Joomla due to the built-in menu editor.

I learned that WordPress uses a page-based structure, while Joomla relies more on articles and modules. This difference affects how content is organised and displayed. Joomla felt more structured and flexible in terms of how content connects, while WordPress prioritises ease of use and faster setup. Understanding both approaches helped me see how different CMS platforms solve similar problems in different ways.

Choosing the right theme is important, as it affects how easily the site can be customised. Block-based themes offer more flexibility but can be harder to use, while classic themes provide a simpler and more controlled editing experience. This showed me that selecting tools based on usability and project needs is an important part of development.

## Interview Reflection
During the group formation activity, I participated in short interview-style conversations with classmates to understand their working styles and expectations. Some of the questions discussed included availability for meetings, preferred communication methods, experience with web development tools, and how each person approaches deadlines and teamwork.

From these discussions, several important points stood out. Clear communication and consistent availability were seen as essential for a successful group. It was also important that all members contribute fairly and stay organised with tasks. When discussing the website project, we agreed that the site should be simple, user-friendly, and clearly communicate its purpose, with easy navigation and consistent design across pages.

Another key point was setting expectations early, such as agreeing on deadlines, tools (e.g. Slack, GitHub), and how to handle disagreements or issues. I also noticed that people who had some prior experience with web development were more confident in explaining their ideas, which highlighted the importance of preparation and understanding your role in a team.

Overall, the interview activity helped me better understand how to evaluate potential team members and what qualities contribute to effective collaboration. It also gave me insight into how professional interviews and team selection processes work in real-world environments.

## Career/Employability/Learning Insights
Learning WordPress is highly valuable for employability, as it is one of the most widely used CMS platforms in industry. Understanding how to create pages, manage themes, and structure content is directly relevant to roles in web development, content management, and digital design.

Comparing WordPress with Joomla gave me a deeper understanding of how different systems are designed and how they handle content and navigation. This ability to quickly learn and adapt to different platforms is an important skill in the workplace, where tools and technologies can vary between projects.

This week also reinforced key professional skills such as adaptability, problem-solving, and independent learning. Choosing between themes, understanding different editing systems, and figuring out how to structure the site required research and experimentation. These are the same types of challenges faced in real-world development work.

The group interview activity also provided valuable employability insights. It showed the importance of communication, teamwork, and setting clear expectations when working in a group. These are essential skills in any IT-related career, especially in collaborative environments.

From this week’s experience, I have concluded that my most effective learning approach is to review guides first, apply the concepts through hands-on practice, and then return to the resources to reinforce my understanding.

# Week 6

## Learning Activities & Resources
This week, I focused on project planning and team organisation for the group assignment. We are using tools we are already familiar with from other subjects and are which used together in a real world team environment. We used GitHub to set up and manage our group repository, ensuring all members had access and that the project structure was ready for collaboration. 

We set up a Trello board using the provided template and reviewed how task management systems can be adapted to suit different workflows. For communication, although Slack was provided, our group primarily used Discord. A large part of this week involved reviewing the assignment requirements (which were released later than expected) and discussing with the team how we should approach the project. This included early planning around tools, structure, and responsibilities.

Another key part of this week was dealing with team communication challenges. Not all members were active in the early stages, despite multiple attempts to reach out through messages and emails. This slowed down initial planning and made it harder to move forward as a full group. As a result, most of the early discussion and setup was handled by a smaller number of active members. This highlighted how important consistent communication and participation are in team projects.

## Content Insights
This week helped me understand how development workflows change when working in a team compared to working individually. While I was already familiar with tools like GitHub and Trello, using them in a group context introduced new challenges.

One key insight was that tools alone do not guarantee good organisation. Even with GitHub and Trello set up correctly, progress depends on team participation and communication. In our case, not all members were active initially, which slowed down early planning. This showed that team engagement is just as important as technical setup.

I also learned that project planning is often uncertain at the beginning. Since the full assignment requirements were released late, we had to start planning without complete information. This required flexibility and ongoing discussion to adjust our approach as new details became available.

Another important insight was the role of task distribution. Breaking down the project into smaller tasks and assigning responsibility is necessary to make progress. Without clear ownership, tasks can be delayed or overlooked.

## Career/Employability/Learning Insights
This week gave me a more realistic view of how team projects work in real-world environments. Even when everyone has the technical skills, challenges such as communication issues, delayed participation, and unclear requirements can impact progress.

I learned the importance of taking initiative in a team. When some members were unresponsive, I continued contributing by helping set up the repository, organising the Trello board, and keeping discussions moving. This is an important professional skill, as developers are often expected to stay productive even when situations are not ideal.

This experience also showed that flexibility is important. Plans can change, requirements can be unclear, and teams may not always function perfectly. Being able to adapt and continue making progress is a key employability skill.

Overall, this week improved my understanding of teamwork, responsibility, and real project workflows. It showed me that successful development is not just about coding, but also about communication, organisation, and consistency across the team.
